One bag, one coat

Packing

Pack ultralight. Malaysia is hot and humid (28–33°C), Japan in October is mild (13–21°C). Layer with the travel coat and buy cheap clothes locally.

🎒 Your 3-piece carry-on setup
1. Travel coat (worn) — pockets carry electronics, docs, snacks. Counts as worn clothing, not a bag. 2. Overhead bag — your main bag in the bin, kept under 7kg (tightest limit across all your flights). 3. Backpack — personal item under the seat. Keep it packable/compressible so it fits. All three fly for free on every leg.
Getting around

Local Transport

IC cards and apps do most of the heavy lifting. Overnight buses in Japan save a fortune.

🇲🇾 Malaysia

  • Touch'n'Go card — tap for KL trains, buses, tolls
  • Grab app — cheap rides everywhere (RM8–20)
  • ETS train KL → Penang (Butterworth) ~RM35
  • AirAsia domestic — Langkawi → KL ~RM60
  • Ferry to Langkawi from Kuala Perlis/Penang ~RM23
  • KLIA Ekspres airport ↔ KL Sentral ~RM55 return

🇯🇵 Japan

  • ICOCA / Suica IC card — tap all trains, buses, conbini
  • Nankai train Kansai (KIX) → Namba, Osaka ~¥930
  • Overnight highway bus Kyoto → Tokyo ~¥3,000
  • Tokyo Metro day pass ~¥600 (24h)
  • Kyoto city bus day pass ~¥700
  • Narita Express (N'EX) Tokyo → Narita ~¥3,070

🚉 Transport Checklist

  • Touch'n'Go card loaded on arrival KL
  • Grab app installed + payment added
  • ETS train KL→Penang booked (KTMB site)
  • Langkawi ferry/flight times checked
  • ICOCA/Suica added to phone wallet (or buy at KIX)
  • Kyoto→Tokyo night bus booked (Willer, Oct 23)
  • Tokyo→Narita N'EX time planned for Oct 30
  • Nankai KIX→Namba arrival transfer noted

Cash & cards

Money

You'll spend in Ringgit (MYR) in Malaysia and Yen (JPY) in Japan. One card handles both at the best rate — here's the setup.

💡 The one rule
Get a Wise card before you leave. Load AUD, it converts at the real interbank rate (~0.5% fee). Use it to pay directly or withdraw from ATMs. Never exchange cash at home or at airport counters — rates are 5–10% worse.

🇲🇾 Ringgit (MYR)

Rate$1 AUD ≈ RM2.9
Hawker mealRM5–12
ATMMaybank/CIMB in arrivals

Card works at malls & chains. Keep cash for hawkers, markets & Grab rides.

🇯🇵 Yen (JPY)

Rate$1 AUD ≈ ¥96
Ramen¥700–1,200 (~$10)
ATM7-Eleven ATM (always works)

Japan is cash-heavy — temples, small shops & buses often card-free. Always carry ¥5,000+ on you.

🧮 Quick maths

RM10≈ $3.50 AUD
¥1,000≈ $10 AUD
¥100 onigiri≈ $1 AUD

Quick yen trick: drop two zeros and halve it. ¥3,200 ≈ $16.

💳 Money Checklist

  • Wise card ordered & loaded with AUD
  • 2nd backup card packed separately
  • Bank notified of travel dates
  • $100 AUD emergency cash in bag
  • Always pay in LOCAL currency at terminals (not AUD)
  • All home bills auto-paid before leaving
